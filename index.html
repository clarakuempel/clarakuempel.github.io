Clara Kümpel

learning theory · neuroscience-inspired AI
Zürich // London

Hi, I'm Clara. I'm interested in how neural networks and humans learn. I like to apply ideas from neuroscience to machine learning theory and vice versa.

Recently, I am mostly interested in learning dynamics and optimisation to improve learning in neural networks from an efficiency, modular, neuroscience-inspired angle.

During my master's at ETH Zürich, I spent some time studying nonlinear learning at the Gatsby Unit (UCL) in Andrew Saxe's lab and self-supervised learning at Mila with Blake Richards' lab. I am also working on optimisation theory with Jonathan Cornford from the University of Leeds. Before I did a computer science degree with IBM, worked on neurosymbolic AI at the MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab, on computational neuroscience at IBM Research in Yorktown Heights (NY), and studied knowledge graphs and cloud computing in their research labs in Zurich and Stuttgart.

Feel free to reach out if you think we might get along :)
